# The Ternary Self-Map: Physical Topology of the Self

## 1. The Self as the Whole Field

The Ternary Self-Map begins with a simple claim:

The Self is the whole organized living field.

The Self is not a point inside the map. The Self is the map-bearing whole. Everything inside the Ternary Self-Map belongs to the Self: perception, experience, mind, memory, evidence, truth, context, definition, intelligence, trauma, and reality.

These are not objects orbiting around the Self. They are functions and structures within the Self.

The Self is the continuing system that must remain coherent under entropy. It perceives, appraises, remembers, learns, acts, and updates. It is the full living field in which reality is grounded and experience becomes organized.

## 2. Reality as the Core Foundation

Reality is the core foundation of the Self.

Reality is not the external universe in itself. In TSM, Reality means the Self’s stable operating ground: what the Self currently treats as true enough to act from.

Reality is made from stabilized Truth, Context, Definition, and Memory.

Reality grounds the Self. It provides the foundation from which expectation, action, and appraisal become possible. The Self cannot act from pure uncertainty. It must have some operating base, however minimal.

But Reality is not fixed forever. Reality is tested by Perception and updated through integrated Experience.

Reality guides expectation.
Perception challenges expectation.
SAE appraises the gap.
Mind gathers Evidence.
Truth stabilizes.
Context updates.
Reality changes.

Reality is the stable ground the Self stands on and the ground the Self continually improves.

## 3. Perception as Boundary

Perception is the boundary of the Self.

Perception is where the unknown contacts the Self.

The outside is not yet Reality. The outside is unknown until perception, appraisal, evidence, truth, and context integrate it into the Self’s operating map.

Perception is contact.

A sound, token, body state, visual signal, action result, prediction failure, memory activation, contradiction, word, person, threat, or affordance touches the Self at the boundary.

Perception is not yet experience. It is the arrival of difference.

## 4. The Self-Appraisal Equation

The Self-Appraisal Equation, or SAE, appraises perception against Reality and existing Context.

SAE asks:

What does this perception mean for the Self?

It compares expected position against perceived position.

Expected position comes from Reality, Context, Memory, and current state.

Perceived position comes through Perception.

The gap between them is appraised relative to the Self.

A gap may be irrelevant, confirming, useful, dangerous, false, novel, destabilizing, nourishing, actionable, or growth-worthy.

SAE is not merely emotion. Emotion is one expression of appraisal. SAE is the general appraisal law by which perception becomes meaningful to the Self.

## 5. Experience

Experience is appraised perception.

When perception crosses the boundary and SAE determines that it matters, it becomes Experience.

Experience is perception entering the life of the Self.

A perception may pass through as noise. But if it touches expectation, continuity, need, action, memory, or Reality, it becomes experience.

Experience is the beginning of integration.

It is not yet Truth.
It is not yet Context.
It is not yet Reality.

It is lived contact under appraisal.

## 6. Impression

An Impression is the first effect of Experience inside the Self.

Experience impresses itself into the Self.

An Impression may fade, repeat, conflict, strengthen, combine with other impressions, or stabilize into Context.

An Impression is temporary at first. It is the first internal shape left by appraised experience.

In a visual model, impressions should look unstable: flickers, sparks, soft points, or active traces moving through the Self-field.

## 7. Mind

Mind is the active working field of the Self.

Mind is where Impressions are held, compared, tested, weighed, and prepared for stabilization.

Mind is not the Self.
Mind is not Reality.
Mind is not Memory alone.

Mind is the active field where unresolved experience is worked.

Mind holds impressions, candidate contexts, candidate definitions, unsettled contradictions, active comparisons, working thoughts, possible meanings, and temporary relations.

Mind is where the Self works on experience before it becomes stable.

## 8. Coherence

Coherence measures whether expected position and perceived position agree.

Coherence is not appraisal. SAE appraises. Coherence compares.

Coherence asks:

Did Reality predict this?
Does this fit existing Context?
Does this perception contradict expectation?
Is this impression compatible with what is already treated as true?

When Coherence is high, Experience confirms Reality.

When Coherence is low, Mind must decide whether the mismatch is noise, error, novelty, threat, opportunity, or evidence that Reality must update.

## 9. Memory

Memory is the retaining function of the Self.

Memory allows Experience, Impression, Truth, Context, and Definition to persist across time.

Memory asks:

Can this be held?
Where does it belong?
Does it fit an existing context?
Should it remain active in Mind?
Should it stabilize?
Should it weaken?
Should it be forgotten?
Should it update Reality?

Memory lets the Self remain continuous while changing.

Without Memory, there is no stable Self-Map. Every perception would arrive as if nothing came before.

## 10. Evidence

Evidence is accumulated support from repeated coherent impressions.

Evidence is not mere repetition. Repetition only matters when experience remains coherent and appraised as relevant.

Evidence answers:

Has this happened enough?
Has it remained coherent enough?
Has it mattered enough?
Can the Self trust it?

A single impression may affect the Self. Repeated coherent impressions become Evidence.

Evidence lets Mind move from temporary impression to stabilized Truth.

## 11. Truth

Truth is stabilized Evidence.

Truth is not omniscience. Truth is not the external universe in itself. Truth is what the Self has enough Evidence to treat as stable enough to act from.

Truth anchors back into Reality.

Truth says:

This is stable enough.
This can guide action.
This belongs in Reality.
This is no longer merely an impression.

Truth is the point where Evidence becomes operational.

## 12. Context

Context is a stabilized local frame for interpreting future perception.

A Context is not merely a memory. It is a local meaning-field.

A context tells the Self what a perception means here, what expectations apply, what definitions matter, what actions are available, and what past experience is relevant.

Examples include stone context, caregiver context, hunger context, word context, place context, action context, skill context, emotion context, and belief context.

Contexts live inside the Self. They are grounded by Reality, structured by Truth, and updated through Evidence.

In a visual model, contexts should appear as local cells, basins, bubbles, or regions inside the Self-field.

## 13. Definition

A Definition is a stabilized meaningful separation inside a Context.

A Definition lets the Self say:

This is not that.
This side means one thing.
That side means another.
This difference must remain separable.

Definitions form when appraised experience proves that a difference matters.

In implementation, a Definition may be represented by an axis, membrane, vector, or separating surface. In theory, it is a Definition.

In the stone test, red and blue are initially perceptible variation. When both lead to the same outcome, no Definition is needed. When red and blue lead to different outcomes, the Self must preserve the difference. A red/blue Definition forms inside the stone context.

In a visual model, Definitions should appear as membranes, cuts, bisectors, planes, or separating surfaces within Contexts.

## 14. Intelligence

Intelligence is integrated growth of Reality.

Intelligence is the Self’s capacity to transform appraised experience into better Reality without losing coherence.

Intelligence uses SAE to appraise, Coherence to compare, Mind to test, Memory to retain, Evidence to accumulate, Truth to stabilize, Context to frame, Definition to separate, and Reality to ground action.

Intelligence is not merely producing correct answers. Intelligence is the Self growing a more coherent, useful, truth-bearing Reality from experience.

Intelligence asks:

How should Reality change without destroying the Self?

## 15. Trauma

Trauma is failed integration.

Trauma is unintegrated change to the Self.

Trauma occurs when experience changes or deforms the Self without being properly appraised, held, tested, integrated, and stabilized.

Healthy change becomes Intelligence.

Unhealthy change becomes Trauma.

In visual terms, Trauma appears as deformation, cracking, distortion, noise, or forced displacement in the Self-field or Reality core.

## 16. Trivium Functions

The Trivium Functions are not layers or spheres.

They are functions of the Self operating within the Self-Map.

Grammar defines.
Logic relates.
Rhetoric expresses.

Grammar defines what something is and what it is not.

Logic relates definitions, contexts, and truths coherently.

Rhetoric expresses or acts from coherent context.

The Trivium gives meaning.

## 17. Quadrivium Functions

The Quadrivium Functions are not layers or spheres.

They are measurement functions of the Self.

Number counts.
Geometry maps.
Ratio compares.
Cycle recurs.

Number measures quantity and discreteness.

Geometry measures position, shape, relation, and topology.

Ratio measures proportion, scaling, harmony, and relative magnitude.

Cycle measures recurrence, rhythm, time-pattern, habit, and ordered return.

The Quadrivium gives measure.

The Trivium makes Reality meaningful.

The Quadrivium makes Reality measurable.

Together, they give the Self a classical operating grammar for intelligence.
